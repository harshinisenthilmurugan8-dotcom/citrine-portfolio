# The Art of Making People Laugh

Stand-up comedy is the most terrifying and rewarding thing I do. You stand alone on a stage, armed with nothing but words, and you try to make a room full of strangers laugh.

## Finding My Voice

Every comedian starts by imitating their heroes. I was no different. But somewhere between my first open mic and my hundredth, I found my own voice. It lives at the intersection of languages, cultures, and the everyday absurdities of Indian life.

## Jokes Blend Of Languages

India is beautifully multilingual, and humor doesn't always translate. So I stopped trying to translate it. "Jokes Blend Of Languages" was born from the idea that comedy is funniest when it's authentic — mixing Tamil, Hindi, English, and everything in between.

## Everything is Political

At some point, I realized that everything worth talking about has a political dimension. Not in the boring, partisan way — but in the way that politics touches our daily lives, our relationships, and our dreams.

> "The best comedy doesn't just make you laugh — it makes you think."

## What I've Learned

- **Vulnerability is strength** — The funniest bits come from the most honest places
- **Read the room** — But also have the courage to challenge it
- **Fail forward** — A joke that bombs teaches you more than one that kills

The stage is calling. Always.
